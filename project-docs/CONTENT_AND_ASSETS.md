# Purpose
This document tracks the content, photography, brand assets, and business details needed from the client for the LensByLey website redesign. It should be treated as the main asset collection checklist unless replaced by a newer approved revision.

# Asset Collection Goal
Collect the strongest possible materials so the redesigned site feels premium, cohesive, and image-led.

The quality of the final website will depend heavily on:
- the quality of the photography
- the consistency of the photo edit
- the strength of the written content
- the completeness of the trust and business materials

# Needed From Client

## Photography
- top 80 to 120 strongest photos
- grouped by category
- full-resolution original files or highest-quality exports only
- no screenshots
- no Instagram downloads
- no Facebook downloads
- no compressed text-message versions

### Photo Categories Needed
- Weddings
- Engagements & Proposals
- Bridal Showers
- Sweet 16s
- Communions
- Portraits
- Private Events

### Hero Images
Need 8 to 12 hero-quality images that can carry large sections or homepage moments.

### Full Event Galleries
Need 5 to 10 complete shoots or events that show consistency and storytelling.

## Brand Assets
- logo files
- transparent PNG
- SVG or vector if available
- watermark files if any
- existing fonts if any
- existing brand colors if any

## Copy and Brand Input
- Leyla bio in her own words
- preferred way to present name and brand
- confirmation on motto usage
- services she wants emphasized
- services she wants de-emphasized
- words she wants the brand to feel like
- words she does not want associated with the brand

## Testimonials and Trust
- at least 6 to 10 testimonials
- client names if approved
- event type and location if approved
- venue list
- planner relationships if relevant
- publications or features if any
- repeat-client stories if any

## Business Information
- current email address
- phone number
- Instagram and other social links
- service area
- response time expectations
- booking process basics
- general pricing direction
- whether starting prices should appear publicly

## Legal / Permissions / Technical
- permission status for featured client images
- domain access
- hosting/platform info
- CRM or form tool preference if any
- Google Business Profile access if relevant

# Quality Rules
- only use high-resolution images
- prioritize editing consistency
- avoid weak filler work
- do not include any image just to fill space
- if a category is weak, use fewer images rather than weaker ones
- curation matters more than volume
