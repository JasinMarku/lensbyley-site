# Purpose
This document defines the page structure, navigation, content architecture, and section-level purpose for the LensByLey website redesign. It should be treated as the site structure source of truth unless replaced by a newer approved revision.

# Sitemap

- Home
- Portfolio
- Services
- About
- Contact

This is a minimal 5-page site. No extra pages should be added unless there is a clear strategic reason.

# Navigation

## Primary Navigation
- Portfolio
- Services
- About
- Contact

Homepage logo/brand should link back to Home.

## Navigation Goals
- clean
- minimal
- easy to scan
- premium without feeling sparse
- no clutter
- no extra links that dilute the main user journey

# Footer Structure

Footer should include:
- brand name
- short brand descriptor
- navigation links
- social links
- email
- phone
- location/service area
- copyright
- optional small designer/developer credit if desired

Do not overload the footer with unnecessary links.

# Page Architecture

## Home

### Purpose
Create the strongest first impression, establish the brand tone, show the range and quality of the work, and guide users toward inquiry.

### Visitor Mindset
- first-time visitor
- evaluating taste and credibility
- deciding whether this feels professional and worth inquiring about

### Homepage Priorities
1. visual impression
2. brand tone
3. quality of photography
4. trust and credibility
5. clear next step

### Recommended Sections
1. Hero
2. Brand intro / about preview
3. Selected work / featured portfolio
4. Services preview
5. Testimonial preview
6. Inquiry CTA
7. Footer

### What Home Should Emphasize
- warm premium brand feel
- range across milestone events
- Leyla as a trusted creative professional
- inquiry-first conversion

### What Should Stay Off Home
- pricing cards
- too many testimonials
- detailed FAQ
- overly long process explanations
- cluttered gallery dumps
- anything that makes the homepage feel like a template

---

## Portfolio

### Purpose
Show the quality, range, and consistency of the photography in a curated, editorial way.

### Visitor Mindset
- wants proof of taste and consistency
- wants to see whether Leyla photographs their kind of event well
- may be comparing her to other photographers

### Portfolio Goals
- make the work feel premium
- show breadth without chaos
- create confidence across different categories
- prove consistency of editing and quality

### Recommended Structure
1. Intro heading and short framing text
2. Mixed editorial portfolio preview grid
3. Category organization beneath that

### Recommended Categories
- Weddings
- Engagements & Proposals
- Showers, Sweet 16s & Communions
- Portraits
- Events

This can be handled with category sections on a single page or elegant filtering, depending on implementation quality.

### Image Strategy
- tightly curated
- avoid too many images
- focus on strongest work only
- keep crops and spacing intentional
- ensure editing consistency

### What Should Stay Off Portfolio
- carousels
- random category mixing without structure
- excessive captions
- visual clutter
- low-quality or inconsistent images

---

## Services

### Purpose
Explain what Leyla offers, who each service is for, and how the experience feels without falling into transactional package-page energy.

### Visitor Mindset
- wants to know whether Leyla handles their event type
- wants clarity without feeling sold to aggressively
- wants to understand whether the service level fits their expectations

### Recommended Sections
1. Intro / philosophy
2. Service category blocks
3. Small proof/testimonial moment if needed
4. CTA to contact

### Recommended Service Categories
- Weddings
- Engagements & Proposals
- Showers / Sweet 16s / Communions
- Portraits
- Private Events

### Content Approach
Each category should include:
- short paragraph
- event-specific point of view
- optional representative image
- optional starting point language if appropriate

### What Should Stay Off Services
- tiered package cards
- checkmark comparison tables
- crowded pricing details
- overly sales-heavy language

---

## About

### Purpose
Build trust, human connection, and personal credibility.

### Visitor Mindset
- wants to know who Leyla is
- wants reassurance that the brand is real, personal, and trustworthy
- wants to understand how she sees and photographs moments

### Recommended Sections
1. Hero / intro portrait
2. First-person story
3. Approach / philosophy
4. Personal trust details
5. CTA to inquire

### About Page Goals
- personal without feeling casual
- warm without being overly sentimental
- clear point of view
- strong portrait-led presentation

### What Should Stay Off About
- generic bio filler
- long timeline resume format
- weak stock-feeling headshots
- vague language that says nothing

---

## Contact

### Purpose
Convert interest into serious inquiries with a calm, polished, easy-to-use inquiry experience.

### Visitor Mindset
- interested enough to reach out
- wants the process to feel easy and professional
- wants to know they are contacting a real business

### Recommended Sections
1. Intro heading
2. short invitation text
3. inquiry form
4. direct email and phone as secondary contact options
5. service area / response expectations

### Inquiry Form Priorities
- clear
- polished
- not intimidating
- not too long
- focused on quality leads

### Suggested Form Fields
- name
- email
- phone optional
- event type
- event date
- venue or location
- how they found LensByLey
- short message

### What Should Stay Off Contact
- overly long form
- cluttered layout
- too much explanatory text
- a phone-first or call-now tone
