# Purpose
This document defines the overall purpose of the LensByLey website redesign and should be treated as the high-level project source of truth unless replaced by a newer approved revision.

# Project Overview

## Client
Leyla Mustofoski

## Brand
LensByLey

## Motto
Capturing your lifetime memories

## Project Summary
Redesigning LensByLey from a dated single-page photography website into a modern, editorial, image-led multi-page brand site that feels warm, polished, credible, and commercially effective.

The new site should present Leyla as a premium NYC/NJ photographer for meaningful events and milestones, including weddings, engagements, proposals, bridal showers, Sweet 16s, communions, portraits, and private events.

This is not a small facelift. It is a full repositioning of the brand's online presence.

## Business Goal
Increase the quality of inquiries across the full mix of services by making the site feel more elevated, trustworthy, and visually intentional.

The site should help:
- attract better-fit clients
- improve first impressions
- create stronger referral confidence from planners, venues, and event professionals
- support repeat clients across multiple life milestones
- move away from a budget-template perception

## Current Site Problems
The current site is working against the quality of the work.

Main issues:
- reads like a template-era single-page site
- weak premium positioning
- phone-first CTA structure
- no proper inquiry flow
- no strong portfolio architecture
- no strong trust signals
- no real separation of services
- generic messaging
- weak image presentation
- low-end visual cues that reduce credibility

## Redesign Goals
The redesigned website should:
- feel editorial, warm, modern, and premium
- remain broad enough to support multiple event categories
- use photography as the primary storytelling medium
- improve inquiry-first conversion
- create a stronger portfolio presence
- make the brand feel more established and professional
- stay minimal and intentional, not overbuilt

## Site Scope
Planned sitemap:
- Home
- Portfolio
- Services
- About
- Contact

No additional pages should be added unless there is a clear strategic reason.

## Success Metrics
Success should be measured by:
- stronger inquiry quality
- better-fit event types and clients
- more credible overall brand perception
- stronger referrals from planners and venues
- improved visual consistency and professionalism
- a site that feels custom, polished, and trustworthy

## Project Principles
- photography is the centerpiece
- the design should feel restrained and refined
- every event category should feel equally elevated
- the site should be minimal, not thin
- the brand should feel warm and personal, not generic or overly sentimental
- the site should support business growth without overclaiming luxury positioning
