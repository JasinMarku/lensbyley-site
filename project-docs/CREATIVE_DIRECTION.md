# Purpose
This document defines the brand positioning, creative direction, tone, and non-negotiable design rules for the LensByLey website redesign. It should be treated as the creative source of truth unless replaced by a newer approved revision.

# Strategic Brief

A New York / New Jersey photographer covering the full arc of personal milestones including weddings, engagements, proposals, bridal showers, Sweet 16s, communions, portraits, and private events.

The current site reads as a budget single-page template and under-sells the range and quality of the work. The goal is a repositioning that feels elevated, editorial, and credible without boxing the brand into luxury-wedding-only territory.

The site must do two jobs well:
1. present weddings and larger events with enough polish that high-quality clients take the work seriously
2. present other milestone events with the same level of care, taste, and visual discipline

The through-line is not one event type. The through-line is taste, consistency, warmth, and craft applied to meaningful occasions.

# Positioning

## Core Positioning
A photographer for the moments that matter.

## Positioning Definition
LensByLey should be positioned as an editorial event photographer, not a luxury wedding studio and not a generic generalist.

The brand is unified by a point of view:
- warm
- observed
- quietly composed
- image-led
- refined
- personal
- trustworthy

Weddings and milestone events should feel like peers under one cohesive brand.

# Audience

## Primary
NYC/NJ milestone clients with meaningful events and real budgets who care about how the work feels, not just how much it costs.

## Secondary
Planners, venues, and event professionals who may refer photographers to clients and need the site to feel polished and credible.

## Tertiary
Repeat and lifecycle clients who may return across years and milestones.

## Deprioritized
- price-led shoppers
- low-commitment leads
- clients who only convert from an easy phone number CTA
- bargain-focused inquiries

# Website Principles

1. One brand across every event type
   Weddings, proposals, bridal showers, Sweet 16s, communions, portraits, and events must all feel equally elevated.

2. Image first, chrome last
   The photograph is the main storytelling tool. Interface elements should stay restrained.

3. Curate aggressively
   Curation creates authority. Avoid volume-heavy galleries and cluttered sections.

4. Typography does the premium work
   Elegant serif display typography paired with a clean neutral sans serif.

5. Warm neutral palette
   White, beige, off-white, cream, and soft neutrals should shape the site.

6. Inquiry-first conversion
   The site should guide users toward a considered inquiry, not a phone-first interaction.

7. Services should communicate range without tiered package energy
   No Silver, Gold, Platinum structure. No checkmark-heavy package cards.

8. Portfolio should feel editorial, not transactional
   Spacious, curated, and cohesive across all event categories.

9. About should feel real and personal
   Strong portrait, first-person voice, clear point of view.

10. Motion should stay subtle
    Minimal animation only. No flashy transitions or trendy effects.

11. Voice should be warm and specific
    Avoid clichés and overly sentimental copy.

12. Mobile should feel designed, not collapsed
    The site should be elegant and intentional on mobile.

13. Proof should be used with restraint
    Testimonials should feel credible and human, not performative.

14. Editing consistency is non-negotiable
    The photography must feel tonally and visually cohesive.

# Design Language

## Visual Tone
- editorial
- warm
- modern
- premium
- soft
- polished
- calm
- minimal
- image-led

## Color Direction
Primary palette should be built around:
- white
- off-white
- cream
- beige
- warm neutrals

Accent usage:
- subtle only
- muted earthy tones if needed
- no loud colors
- no harsh contrast

## Typography Direction
- elegant serif for headlines
- restrained sans serif for body copy and navigation
- timeless and premium
- not trendy
- no more than two typefaces total

## Layout Direction
- generous whitespace
- strong image hierarchy
- asymmetrical editorial rhythm where helpful
- full-bleed used intentionally, not excessively
- clean sections with breathing room

# Things to Avoid

Do not introduce:
- cheesy wedding-site styling
- clipart icons
- gradients
- glassmorphism
- heavy shadows
- startup/SaaS styling
- package cards
- testimonial carousels
- star ratings
- vendor-badge clutter
- social-media-grid energy
- busy collage layouts
- generic photographer template patterns
- overly poetic or vague section language
- overclaimed luxury language

Avoid words like:
- luxury
- magical
- dream
- your special day
- love story
- capture forever

# Creative Direction Lock

Brand: A New York / New Jersey photographer covering weddings, engagements, proposals, showers, Sweet 16s, communions, portraits, and private events. Repositioning from a budget single-page template into an elevated, editorial, image-led brand.

Positioning: A photographer for the moments that matter. Editorial event photographer, not a luxury wedding studio and not a generic generalist. Unified by a point of view that is warm, observed, quietly composed, and refined.

Audience: NYC/NJ milestone clients investing in meaningful events, planners and venues who refer, and repeat clients across multiple milestones.

Sitemap: Home, Portfolio, Services, About, Contact.

Design language:
- Editorial serif display with one restrained neutral sans serif
- Warm neutral palette built around white, beige, off-white, cream, and soft neutrals
- Generous whitespace
- Strong image hierarchy
- Asymmetrical editorial structure where useful
- Minimal UI
- Subtle motion only
- No clipart, package cards, gradients, glassmorphism, vendor badges, or template-level design cues

Services treatment:
- Organized by event type
- No tiered packages
- No checkmark lists
- Short point-of-view copy
- Starting price or custom quote language can be used carefully if needed

Portfolio treatment:
- Curated editorial landing presentation
- Organized by category
- No carousel-heavy or cluttered presentation
- Tight image selection only

Voice:
- first person
- warm
- specific
- uncomplicated
- personal but polished

Conversion model:
- inquiry-first
- contact form is primary
- phone is secondary and should not dominate the experience

One-brand rule:
Every service category must feel equally elevated. No downgraded visual treatment for non-wedding work.

Non-negotiables:
- curated image counts
- editing consistency
- premium restraint
- strong mobile experience
- cohesive visual language across all pages

Success metric:
Inquiry quality, referral confidence, and stronger premium perception across the full event mix.
